When a supply is **exempt** or **outside the scope** of VAT. Anchors are citable as
`[skill: vat-scope#anchor]`. Reference only — never a figure source (CONTRACT §8 A1, A3).

## Exempt supplies {#exempt}
- treatments: exempt
- keywords: insurance, insurance premium, indemnity, financial services, loan interest, bank interest, residential rent, postage, health services
- rule: Exempt supplies include insurance, most financial services and loan interest, the rent of residential property (unless an option to tax applies), postage, and certain health/education services. Exempt supplies carry no VAT and are excluded from Box 6 — this is different from zero-rated, which is taxable at 0%.

## Outside the scope of VAT {#outside-scope}
- treatments: outside_scope
- keywords: salary, wages, payroll, staff wages, dividend, dividends, drawings, internal transfer, intercompany transfer, grant, gift, statutory
- rule: Payments that are not consideration for a supply are outside the scope of VAT entirely — employee salaries and wages, dividends, owner drawings, purely internal transfers, and most grants. These never appear in any VAT box.

## Option to tax on property {#option-to-tax}
- treatments: standard, exempt
- keywords: commercial rent, office rent, opted to tax, option to tax, rent of premises, business rates
- rule: Rent of commercial property is normally exempt, but a landlord may 'opt to tax', making it standard-rated at 20%. If a rent transaction is classified standard, confirm an option to tax is in place; if classified exempt, confirm one is NOT in place.
