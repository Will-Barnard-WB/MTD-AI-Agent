Rules for the VAT Flat Rate Scheme (FRS). Used by the scheme router (Phase B) and citable
as `[skill: flat-rate-scheme#anchor]`. Reference only — never a figure source.

## Flat Rate Scheme basics {#basics}
- treatments: standard
- keywords: flat rate, flat-rate scheme, frs
- rule: Under the Flat Rate Scheme a business pays a fixed percentage of its VAT-inclusive turnover to HMRC and does not reclaim input VAT on most purchases. It is open to businesses with taxable turnover up to £150,000 (excl. VAT). Box 1 is the flat percentage applied to gross turnover; Box 4 is normally nil.

## Limited cost business {#limited-cost}
- treatments: standard
- keywords: limited cost, labour only, low goods
- rule: A 'limited cost business' — one spending very little on goods (below 2% of turnover, or below a small quarterly floor) — must use a higher 16.5% flat rate. Labour-only and consultancy businesses are frequently limited cost.

## Capital asset input VAT {#capital-assets}
- treatments: standard
- keywords: capital asset, equipment over 2000, single purchase
- rule: The one exception to 'no input VAT reclaim' under FRS is a single purchase of capital goods costing £2,000 or more (VAT-inclusive), on which input VAT may be reclaimed in Box 4.
